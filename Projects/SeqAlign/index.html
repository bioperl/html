Project: Sequence Alignment
Sequence alignments are a major part of the computational analysis of sequence information. The requirements of different researcheers has necessitated the creation of two different sequence alignment objects. This is an area of active research and the concepts of a "lightweight" and "heavyweight" alignment object are still being refined.
  • SimpleAlign.pm

  • UnivAln.pm
Current Discussions

At ISMB-99, George Hartzell (hartzell@cs.berkeley.edu) volunteered to lead a discussion of what the next generation alignment objects should look like. Stay tuned to the guts mailing list for the beginning of the fireworks.
