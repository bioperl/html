About the bioperl project
The Bioperl Project is an international association of developers of open source Perl tools for bioinformatics, genomics and life science research.

We work closely with our friends and colleagues at biojava.org, biopython.org, bioxml.org and biocorba.org. .

The Bioperl server provides an online resource for modules, scripts, and web links for developers of Perl-based software for life science research. We can also provide web, FTP and CVS space for individuals and organizations wishing to distribute or otherwise make freely available standalone scripts & code.

Getting Started
Just learning about perl and/or computational biology?

  • Visit bioinformatics.org
  • Read Lincoln Stein's article, "How Perl Saved the Human Genome Project"
  • Join a Bioperl mailing list
  • Read the The Perl Journal
  • Visit perl.com or The Perl Institute
  • Find a local users group
  • The BITS Online Journal tackles bioinformatics from an information technology (IT) perspective.

Getting Together
The first official Bioperl workshop was held in Heidelberg Germany on August 5th 1999 conjunction with ISMB'99. Check out some pictures. The success of the bioperl-99 gathering has lead to our involvement with other events including the very successful Bioinformatics Open Source Conference (BOSC'2000). Since then our BOSC meetings have become officially recognized sister conferences of the much larger ISMB meetings.

Volunteers needed
Bioperl is run by volunteers from all over the world, with many types of backgrounds. Any contributions of Perl code, html, documentation writing or code testing are very welcome. We are specifically looking for people to do the following jobs (they should not take more than 1-2 hours a week).
  • Open Bio web master
  • Cookbook documentation writers
Currently these responsibilities are being shared by the core group and we feel that they can be easy, small projects for people looking to contribute to bioperl.
