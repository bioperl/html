About the bioperl project
The Bioperl Project is an international association of developers of open source Perl tools for bioinformatics, genomics and life science research.

We work closely with our friends and colleagues at biojava.org, biopython.org, bioxml.org and biocorba.org. .

The Bioperl server provides an online resource for modules, scripts, and web links for developers of Perl-based software for life science research. We can also provide web, FTP and CVS space for individuals and organizations wishing to distribute or otherwise make freely available standalone scripts & code.

News

[August / September 2000]

  • Pictures from BOSC'2000 are online. We are working on getting the presentations and proceedings online as well. Send comments, corrections and additional pictures to dag@sonsorol.org.

  • The Bioperl website is in the process of becoming Wiki-enabled. We hope the collaborative editing and authoring features will keep the website more topical, accurate and up-to-date. To see a Wiki site in action check out the EnsemblDocs Wiki.

  • Peter van Heusden is coordinating the release of Bioperl 0.6.2 which is a bug fix release of Bioperl 0.6.1. This should be available in September. The main CVS branch of Bioperl contains significant new code and functionality that will *NOT* be part of the 0.6.2 release. We are working on coordinating a Bioperl 0.7 release that will merge all the new features into the main distribution. We estimate that Bioperl 0.7 will come out in October or November. Scanning the bioperl-l archives is a good way to see what new code and features have been added.

  • Bioperl has been added to the FreeBSD ports tree! Thanks to Johann Visagie for putting this together.

  • A new sister site biocorba.org was launched during BOSC'2000. Jason Stajich is leading the effort.

  • Our Bioinformatics Open Source Conference (BOSC'2000) was a success with more than 220 attendees. Pictures and presentations will be up shortly.

Getting Together
The first official Bioperl workshop was held in Heidelberg Germany on August 5th 1999 conjunction with ISMB'99. Check out some pictures.

The success of the bioperl-99 gathering has lead to our involvement with BOSC'2000 which will be held right before ISMB'2000 in August.

Bioperl people have been traditionally spotted at the following meetings:

  • International Conference on Intelligent Systems for Molecular Biology (ISMB)
  • Objects in Bioinformatics (OiB). Previous OiB meetings: OiB98, OiB97

Volunteers needed
Bioperl is run by volunteers from all over the world, with many types of backgrounds. Any contributions of Perl code, html, documentation writing or code testing are very welcome. We are specifically looking for people to do the following jobs (they should not take more than 1-2 hours a week).
  • FAQ maintainers
  • Cookbook documentation writers
Currently these responsibilities are being shared by the core group and we feel that they can be easy, small projects for people looking to contribute to bioperl.
