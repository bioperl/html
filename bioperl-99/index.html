Announcing the first official bioperl workshop!

The meeting will be held on the thursday before the start of ISMB'99,
just before the start of the tutorial sessions.

Visit the official ISMB site at http://ismb99.gmd.de

 

Purpose & Scope
The workshop is a one day meeting for people active or interested in using perl for life science research. There will be a significant focus on the current work and future plans of the Bioperl consortium including demos and examples of our freely available bioperl modules in use around the world. The workshop is not limited to bioperl members or developers -- anyone using perl to solve interesting life science problems is welcome to attend and submit proposals for talks and poster presentations.

The format of the meeting will be a mixture of verbal presentations and poster sessions during the day with two keynote speakers:

  • Steve Chervits, Bioperl coordinator
  • Lincoln Stein, Author of CGI.pm and Aceperl

Facilities
The workshop will be held at the German Cancer Research Center (DKFZ) in Heidelberg which is a short bus ride away from the main ISMB hotels and facilities. Attendees are expected to make their own arrangements for travel and accommodation (we assume most will simply extend their bookings for ISMB).

Fees
We have elected to charge a small fee to cover the incedental costs of organizing the workshop. Registration fees will be $30 for students/postdocs, $50 for other academics and $70 for industry or commercial attendees. All fees payable in US dollars. Registration includes coffee, tea, lunch and all workshop materials.
